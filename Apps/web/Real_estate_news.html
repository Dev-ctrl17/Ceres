Land Investment in Nigeria: Strategic Capital Banking

Strategic land banking continues to be one of the most reliable and time-tested methods for generating outsized capital gains within the Nigerian property sector. This approach involves acquiring raw land parcels in the direct path of planned public infrastructure improvements, holding the assets as municipal expansion moves outward, and selling to commercial developers once property demand spikes.

Currently, the most rapid land appreciation corridors are concentrated along major infrastructure networks. Areas directly linked to new international airport projects, deep sea ports, and regional highway expansions are seeing significant value growth. When pursuing land investment in Nigeria, the single most critical factor for success is securing an absolute, undisputed legal title—such as a government-issued Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) or a registered Governor's Consent—to ensure your investment remains fully protected against future zoning changes or ownership disputes.

2. What distinguishes a Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) from a Governor's Consent?
A Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) is the primary document issued directly by the state government to the first person to claim leasehold interest on a land parcel. A Governor's Consent is the mandatory official clearance required for all subsequent transactions, sales, or transfers of that land interest to new owners.
