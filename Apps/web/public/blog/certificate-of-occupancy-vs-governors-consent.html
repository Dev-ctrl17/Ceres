Certificate of Occupancy vs Governor's Consent: Key Differences Explained

Understanding the difference between a Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) and Governor's Consent is essential for anyone buying property in Nigeria. Both documents are legally required, but they serve different purposes.

What is a Certificate of Occupancy (C of O)?

A C of O is the most important land document in Nigeria. It proves that the original owner has legal rights to occupy and develop the land. It's issued by the state government and is valid for 99 years (for residential) or 50 years (for commercial).

What is Governor's Consent?

Governor's Consent is required whenever a property changes hands. It's the legal mechanism that transfers ownership from the seller to the buyer. Without Governor's Consent, the transfer is not legally recognized.

Which Do You Need?

You need both. When buying property, verify the seller has a valid C of O, then file for Governor's Consent to transfer ownership to your name. Never complete a transaction without both documents.
