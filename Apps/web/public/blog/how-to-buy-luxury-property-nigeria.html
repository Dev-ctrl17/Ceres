How to Buy Luxury Property in Nigeria: Step-by-Step Guide 2026

Buying luxury property in Nigeria is a significant investment that requires careful planning, expert guidance, and thorough due diligence. This step-by-step guide walks you through exactly how to buy luxury property in Nigeria — from setting your budget to securing legal ownership.

Step 1: Set Your Budget

Before you start searching, determine your total budget. Remember to include 15-20% above the property price for legal fees (5-10%), agency commission (5%), survey costs, and Governor's Consent fees. Luxury property prices in Lagos range from ₦40 million in Lekki to over ₦5 billion on Banana Island.

Step 2: Engage a Trusted Luxury Real Estate Agency

Partnering with a verified agency like Luxury Properties Ltd — recognized as the best luxury real estate agency Nigeria — gives you access to off-market listings, verified properties, and expert guidance through every step.

Step 3: Conduct Title Due Diligence

This is the most critical step. Your lawyer must verify the Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) at the state land registry. Check for encumbrances, government acquisition, or disputes. This typically takes 5-14 business days.

Step 4: Inspect the Property

Always inspect in person with a structural engineer. Check foundation integrity, electrical systems, plumbing, roofing, and waterproofing. Never buy based solely on photos or videos.

Step 5: Draft and Sign the Deed of Assignment

Your lawyer drafts a Deed of Assignment outlining payment terms, completion timeline, and penalties for default. Both parties sign before witnesses.

Step 6: Make Payment via Escrow

Always use a verified escrow account managed by your legal team. Never pay cash or to personal accounts. This protects both buyer and seller.

Step 7: Secure Governor's Consent

This is the final legal step. File for Governor's Consent at the Lagos State Land Registry to officially transfer ownership. Budget 6-12 weeks and approximately 10% of the property value in fees.
