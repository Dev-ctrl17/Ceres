Best Streets in Ikoyi

Thompson Avenue — The most prestigious street, home to diplomats and CEOs. Properties rarely hit the open market. Bourdillon Road — Wide, tree-lined avenue with luxury mansions. Alexander Avenue — Quiet, exclusive street with high security. Ribadu Road — Prime commercial-residential mix. Queen's Drive — Waterfront properties with lagoon views.

Old Ikoyi vs New Ikoyi

Old Ikoyi (around Thompson Avenue, Bourdillon) is more exclusive with larger properties and stricter security. New Ikoyi (Awolowo Road, Alfred Rewane) is busier with more commercial activity and apartment buildings. Old Ikoyi commands 30-50% higher prices.

Why Invest in Ikoyi?

Ikoyi offers the strongest capital appreciation in Lagos luxury real estate at 20-30% annually. Property values are driven by extreme land scarcity, high-net-worth tenant demand, and the prestige factor. It's the safest long-term luxury investment in Nigeria.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ikoyi more expensive than Victoria Island?

Yes, Ikoyi is generally 20-40% more expensive than Victoria Island for comparable properties, due to its more residential character and higher exclusivity.

Can foreigners buy property in Ikoyi?

Yes, foreigners can buy property in Ikoyi. The process requires a valid passport, TIN, and Nigerian bank account. Luxury Properties Ltd specializes in helping international buyers.
